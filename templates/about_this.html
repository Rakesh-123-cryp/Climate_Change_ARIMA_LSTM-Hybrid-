What Are Heat Waves ?
Heat waves are defined as periods of unusually hot weather accompanied by high humidity caused due to pockets of trapped air. This trapped air gets heated up by the sun and causes an abnormal rise in temperature.
This problem is further intensified due to steady rise in climate change, whose major contributor is the greenhouse gases. These greenhouse gases can be released by vehicles, industries, thermal power plants. As climate change brings up global temperatures, even natural causes like wildfires, stubble burning, and other causes of air pollution can also worsen the situation, even in low emission scenarios. Stagnation of air masses is also usually observed during heat waves, favouring the accumulation of pollutant particles and O3 precursors.
What Is AQI ?
AQI (Air Quality Index) is a measure of the quality of air in a specific location. The AQI values ranges from 0-500, with higher values indicating poorer air quality. It is calculated based on the levels of airborne pollutants present in the atmosphere such as SO2, CO2, CO, NO2, ozone.

High temperatures caused by climate change can cause thermal stress and can worsen air quality. Moreover, the combination of high temperatures and drought can result in forest fires that contribute to the degradation of air quality. Hot weather can also be induced by the transport of hot air masses from neighbouring states and cities that are rich in dust.
Some Of The Assumptions We Have Made Are:
  • We will be ignoring the effects of aerosols as the available data is very little.
  • We will be ignoring the effects of pollution that neighboring states have on the state of Telangana, as the cities that we are trying to formulate predictions for are not near the border, and their effects on our prediction will be very minimal.
  • Since Telangana is not a coastal state, we will be ignoring the consequences of coastal wind patterns and the effects of sea breeze.
  • We assume the data acquired during the period of the COVID pandemic to be an outlier, as it was an unanticipated global event and caused unpredictable patterns of pollution and energy consumption.
